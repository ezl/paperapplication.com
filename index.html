Minimal Incremental Environmental Impact

Now, some of the naysayers will say you're contributing to a pollution problem and that they're going "green" by going digital.

Well you might be using a little paper, but that's a small price to pay for the security of paper. And your marginal impact is VERY low -- here are the numbers.

Approximately 100 million rental applications are processed per year in the United States of America. With an average of 6 pages of physical paper used per process, that's:

  • 600 million sheets of paper (about 15,000,000 pounds)
  • 83,500,000 kilowatt hours of energy used to produce that (enough to power an average American household for 6,250 years)
  • 27,250 metric tons of CO2 emmissions (That's a lot, just trust us)
  • 1.8 BILLION gallons of water used (515 million toilet flushes)
  • 201,000 printer ink cartriges at 3.5 pounds each of solid waste and packaging that go into landfills, taking 600 to 1000 years each to decompose
  • 17.09 million more pounds of solid waste that goes into the production of the paper used
  • Some experts also believe that a few trees get cut down in the process

However, as an independent small company or property manager, your marginal impact is miniscule. Each individual company barely even moves the needle!

Given that there's very little impact you individually can make globally by switching to digital/online applications, there's really no incentive to get off of paper (except for access anywhere, automated reporting, integrated screening, online payment processing of credit cards, no more paper checks and bank runs, etc.)
